Title: 1on1 - Marketing Director Resignation (Exit Interview)
Date: 2026-03-11
Participants: CEO (Alex), Marketing Director (Ben)
----------------------------------------

Alex: Ben, thanks for staying after the all-hands. I know you said it's urgent. Is everything okay?

Ben: Alex, I'll be direct. I'm handing in my resignation. My last day will be the 31st.

Alex: Whoa. Ben. This is... unexpected. We just closed the Q1 numbers. Marketing qualified leads (MQLs) are up 40%. You're killing it. Why now?

Ben: It's not about the numbers, Alex. It's the pace. I haven't seen my kids on a weekend in three months. The pivot to "Enterprise First" was brutal. We rewrote the entire website, launched 5 whitepapers, and did 3 conferences in 6 weeks. I'm burnt out. Completely.

Alex: I get it. We pushed hard. But look, we can hire you a deputy. Someone to take the execution load off. You focus on strategy. Take a week off, right now. Paid. Just unplug.

Ben: I appreciate that, truly. But my wife and I talked. I need a break. A real break. Maybe 6 months. I'm not going to another startup. I'm going to consult part-time, maybe teach.

Alex: (Sighs) Okay. If it's family, I can't argue with that. I respect it. But Ben, we need a smooth transition. The Series B deck relies heavily on your narrative. Can you give me 4 weeks instead of 2?

Ben: 3 weeks. That gets us through the investor roadshow prep. But I can't do the roadshow itself.

Alex: Fair. 3 weeks. Now, about your equity. You're vesting next month. We can accelerate that. Make sure you leave with your full 1-year cliff. You earned it.

Ben: Thanks, Alex. That means a lot. I still believe in Nexus. The product is real. I just... I can't be the one running the marathon anymore.

Alex: I understand. Let's draft a comms plan for the team tomorrow. I don't want panic. We frame it as a "Personal Sabbatical". 

Ben: Agreed. I'll prep the handover docs for Sarah. She can cover interim.

Alex: Yeah. Sarah will love that. (Laughs dryly). Alright, Ben. Let's make these 3 weeks count.
