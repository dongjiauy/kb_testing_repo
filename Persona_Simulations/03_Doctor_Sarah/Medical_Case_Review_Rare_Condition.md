# Case Review: Rare T-Wave Inversion
**Resident Presentation**: Dr. Chen (Internal Medicine)
**Attending Reviewer**: Dr. Sarah (Cardiology)

## Patient Profile
- 28F, Athlete (Marathon Runner).
- Complaint: Palpitations during sleep.

## Findings
- EKG shows deep T-wave inversions in V1-V3.
- Echo: Mildly dilated RV (Right Ventricle).
- Troponin: Normal.

## Differential Diagnosis
- ARVC (Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy)?
- Brugada Syndrome?
- Athlete's Heart (Physiological)?

## Discussion
- Sarah: Wait, did you check her family history?
- Chen: Yes. Uncle died suddenly at 35 while jogging.
- Sarah: That's a huge red flag. This screams genetic cardiomyopathy. Order a cardiac MRI immediately. Look for fatty infiltration in the RV wall. And get a genetic panel for Desmoplakin mutations.
- Chen: Should we admit her?
- Sarah: Yes. Telemetry monitoring. She is at high risk for VT (Ventricular Tachycardia). Do not discharge.
